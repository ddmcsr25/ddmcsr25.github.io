Bypassing FRP and Lock Screen on Pixel 6 Running Android 14

Author: Derek Morris
Published: July 2024
Location: Merced, California

Overview

This research documents a critical Android 14 vulnerability that allows a complete FRP (Factory Reset Protection) bypass and lock screen bypass on the Google Pixel 6, even when the bootloader is locked and USB debugging is turned off.

The method was responsibly disclosed to Google through the Android Security Rewards Program (ASR). The issue was verified, and a reward was issued â€” though no public credit has been provided.

What Makes This Bypass Dangerous?

  • Works on Pixel 6 with Android 14
  • Bootloader locked
  • USB Debugging disabled
  • Factory Reset Protection enabled

Tools used:

  • Pixel Flasher
  • Android Platform Tools
  • Android 15 Beta OTA update

Devices and Builds Affected

Device: Google Pixel 6
Codename: oriole

Summary of Exploit Steps

  1. Reboot into Recovery Mode
  2. Factory reset the device
  3. Enable "Apply Update from ADB"
  4. Connect to Pixel Flasher + ADB sideload
  5. Sideload Android 15 Beta OTA
  6. FRP and lock screen are bypassed
  7. Boot device and gain full access
  8. Enable Developer Options and unlock bootloader
